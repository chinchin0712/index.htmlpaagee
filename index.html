About Me, Page 1

This story also traces back to my childhood—to my life as Cassey. When I was young, I was surrounded by a love that was deep, genuine, and pure, especially from my Lola, my parents, and my siblings. But as time passes, we realize that everything changes. Little by little, as we grow, mature, and become independent, we are met with challenges we never expected. We face regrets, mistakes, and hard lessons—experiences that none of us are truly exempt from. Growing up brings realizations that hit hard; past traumas and insecurities can sometimes make us feel like we aren’t worth it or that we’ll never be enough. But I’ve learned that change is the only constant, and everything will eventually be okay if you find the strength to keep fighting and surviving. Problems may pass, but they often leave echoes behind—haunting our dreams or surfacing through triggers in our daily lives. Memories live forever in our minds, reminding us that no one is perfect, and that is okay. We are all just works in progress.

I’ve always been the type of person who keeps her thoughts tucked away in a quiet corner of her mind. I find it hard to open up completely or share those 'unsaid' feelings because there’s always a voice in my head telling me that people will just judge me, find me too much, or eventually leave. It’s a lonely habit—playing the part of the girl who’s okay with never being heard, even when I have so much to say. I’ll be honest: I absolutely hate the feeling of being ignored. It makes my heart sink. Yet, I’ve realized I sometimes do the exact same thing to others. It’s not because I’m mean, but because the moment I feel like I’m losing my importance to someone, I instinctively pull away. I ignore them first just to protect myself from the pain of being forgotten. I guess I’d rather disappear on my own terms than wait for someone to get tired of me. It’s a defense mechanism, a messy way of dealing with the fear that maybe, just maybe, I don’t matter as much as I want to.

I’d like to think of myself as someone who carries a lot of love, though it often comes with a bit of a heavy heart. I’m naturally kind and deeply caring—I’m the one who’s always checking in on my friends, my relatives, and my family just to make sure they’re okay. But truthfully, I’m also the person who gets hurt by the smallest things. I tend to 'mag-tampo' easily because I value presence so much; being ignored is the one thing I can’t handle. It’s a quiet ache I carry—being the person who looks after everyone, yet feeling like the one who rarely gets asked, 'Are you okay?' or 'How have you been?' I don’t ask for much, just the same warmth I try so hard to give to the world.

Quietly Being Me

Ever since I started my journey at UCC, I’ve slowly found myself embracing the art of being alone. I’ve grown used to the quiet rhythm of commuting by myself, eating at a small table for one, and navigating the busy campus hallways without a constant companion by my side. At first, it felt heavy, but now, I’ve discovered a certain peace in my own company.

I do have friends—faces I see every day and people I talk to—but deep down, it feels different. There’s a hollow space where a 'best friend' should be, and I often feel like I’m just drifting on the sidelines of their lives. It’s hard not to notice when the only time my phone lights up with their names is because they need something from me—a favor, an answer, or a hand to hold.

I see the patterns, and I notice the distance, but I’ve chosen not to let it make me bitter. I don’t take it seriously anymore because I’ve realized that I cannot force a seat at a table where I’m only an afterthought. So, I continue to be quietly me. I stay kind, I remain helpful, but I’ve learned to protect my energy. I am learning that my worth isn’t defined by how many people truly 'know' me, but by how well I know and love myself in the silence. I am my own safe harbor now.
